# Physical Layer

The **Physical Layer** is the lowest layer of the TCP/IP model. It is responsible for transmitting bits from one device to another and is the only layer that has direct contact with the physical world.

The Physical Layer does not interpret the content of the information. It does not work with IP addresses, MAC addresses, ports, or protocols. Its only responsibility is to represent bits as physical signals and transmit them through a communication medium.

To accomplish this, the Physical Layer receives a sequence of bits from the upper layers and converts them into physical signals that can travel across the transmission medium. Depending on the technology being used, these signals may be represented as:

- Electrical impulses (Ethernet over copper)
- Pulses of light (Fiber Optic)
- Radio waves (Wi-Fi)

## Components of the Physical Layer

Some of the most common components associated with the Physical Layer include:

- Ethernet Cables (UTP/STP)
- Fiber Optic Cables
- Coaxial Cables
- Wi-Fi Antennas
- RJ-45 Connectors
- Repeaters
- Hubs
- Network Interface Card (NIC)

> **Note:** The Network Interface Card (NIC) is commonly associated with the Physical Layer because it converts bits into physical signals and vice versa. However, it also performs functions at the Data Link Layer, such as handling MAC addresses and processing Ethernet frames.
