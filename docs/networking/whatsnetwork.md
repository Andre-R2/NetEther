# What is a data network?

## Overview

A data network is a collection of interconnected devices that exchange data and share resources. Its primary purpose is to enable communication between devices so information can be exchanged efficiently.

## Hosts and endpoints

The end devices, also known as hosts or endpoints, are responsible for generating, sending, receiving, and consuming the information that travels across the network. Examples include computers, laptops, servers, smartphones, and Internet of Things (IoT) devices.

## Network devices

As technology evolved and networks became larger and more complex, direct communication between devices was no longer sufficient. This led to the introduction of network devices — specialized equipment responsible for managing, maintaining, and protecting communication between hosts through network hardware and configurations.

Examples of network devices include routers, switches, firewalls, and access points. These devices make it possible to build networks that are more efficient, scalable, and fault tolerant.

## A brief origin

The origin of modern computer networks dates back to the late 1960s with the development of ARPANET, the first network to connect universities and research centers for information sharing. Its design introduced fundamental concepts such as redundancy and fault tolerance, principles that continue to play a key role in modern network design.

## Why it matters today

Today, data networks are one of the fundamental pillars of modern technological infrastructure. They enable Internet connectivity, remote work, cloud computing, and many of the digital services used every day.
